Our Story

The Beginning

East Bali Poverty Project (EBPP) is a non-profit organization established in 1998 by a British resident of Bali after an appeal for help by an isolated rural community in the mountains of East Bali.

The surveys conducted revealed thousands of people living in abject poverty without basic facilities (including healthcare), water facilities and electricity. Illiteracy was approaching 100%. Malnutrition and iodine deficiency disorders (IDD) were endemic.

Community-Led Approach

When interviewed in November 1998 to determine their priorities and whether EBPP could help, over 1,000 families requested children's education as a foundation for a better future.

An inspiring and productive collaboration that began with the most disadvantaged communities. Starting from education as the first touch point, we continue to develop various environmental initiatives that harness sustainable organic farming methods...

Our Philosophy

"helping people to help themselves", all programmes are designed to enable total social betterment executed by local people who transfer knowledge directly, and appropriate technology to their communities.

Meet Our People

David J Booth MBE

Founder & Chief Executive Officer

David, a civil engineer from NW England with over 20 years experience in seven countries, moved to Jakarta in 1989.

He relocated to Bali in 1993 where he devoted his life to service to the people of Indonesia, his adopted country, and established the East Bali Poverty Project in 1998 after months of research to find the most disadvantaged region in mountainous East Bali as a region he could apply his extensive experience.

He still coordinates all project planning and design. David was awarded the MBE for "Services to sustainable development in rural East Bali, Indonesia" in Queen Elizabeth II's 2004 Birthday Honours List.

Komang Kurniawan, S.H.

Chairman, Team Leader of all EBPP Field Programs

A native of Tianyar in East Bali and a graduate in Law from Universitas Teknologi Indonesia, Komang is a skilled leader and communicator who partnered with David in 1996.

He was instrumental in initiating culturally sensitive communication with the communities of the 19 remote Ban village hamlets, leading to a partnership of mutual trust.

As Team Leader since 1998, Komang has coordinated all field activities of over 30 projects and managing 120+ field staff in 2008. He currently leads a team of over 40 field staff and various volunteers.

Tri Budiyono

Vice Chairman & Secretariat Administration

Originating from Solo in Java, Tri joined David as volunteer in August 1998 after graduating from Bali's Udayana University and coordinated the interviews of 1,056 families in Desa Ban. His work formed the basis for EBPP's community development blueprint.

Since 1999, he has been the mainstay of all office administration, development and training of all EBPP admin staff.

As Vice Chairman and Secretariat Administrator, Tri leads our Denpasar office team and inspires confidence in all donors and the Yayasan Board of Directors.
