Volunteer Opportunities

Join us in making a difference in East Bali

Foreign applicants for either volunteer or internships

Requirements

  • Minimum of 20 years old.
  • Have relevant university qualifications, in one or more of EBPP fields of activity.
  • Have relevant experience in your field of expertise, such as teaching, health, water & sanitation, agriculture, or civil/social engineering, as well as a current photograph, any published papers, books, articles etc. that may be relevant to your application.
  • Provide a CV/Resume and a statement of purpose (a minimum of one page).
  • Prepared to commit for minimum 2 months, preferably longer. There are special circumstances where we may accept volunteers for shorter periods, but this is at the discretion of EBPP.
  • Provide a reference letter from your university that can demonstrate that the volunteer work at EBPP sponsored social/cultural or environmental project is relevant to your studies.

Other Information

  • We can only accept scholars/interns who enter Indonesia on EBPP-sponsored social/cultural visa.
  • EBPP provides the sponsorship letter and you cannot work on any other project.
  • While you are in Indonesia on EBPP-sponsored social/cultural visa, you cannot work on any other project.
  • Preference will be given to applicants with proven management and/or leadership skills, especially those who have had multicultural vision and ethnic work environments.
  • Internship time-frame: 2 to 6 months.
  • International driving license is required if the volunteers want to rent a car or motor cycle. EBPP does not provide health insurance cover. We advise you to get health insurance from your home country.
  • Follow the rules & regulations of EBPP and always work together with the EBPP staff to achieve the goals of the project.
  • Volunteer must sign our Child Protection Policy, Code of Conduct and Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) once they start with EBPP.
  • Respect the customs & culture of all communities.
  • Respect the individual communities, their individual communities, and understanding that each community may differ.
  • Respect & obey all of the Indonesian Government Laws.
  • Respect the EBPP staff and the communities you are working with.
  • Standard working hours are Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm.
  • We advise volunteers to vaccinate against hepatitis A and B, tetanus and rabies.
  • Living costs: there are low-cost small hotels near our Denpasar office which rate Rp.150,000 - Rp.200,000 per night, including breakfast. If you want to stay in Denpasar, the monthly rent costs Rp.2,000,000 - Rp.3,000,000 for a small house or apartment. If you want to stay in Desa Ban, the accommodation will be based on our project site and only for volunteers from these field of study.

Indonesian Applicants

The same requirements as above except for visa requirements, plus the following:

  • A full Indonesian university and/or diploma license is an advantage.
  • Ability to communicate in English is an advantage.
  • Good command of English, both spoken and written.
  • Willing to work in remote areas.
